Thoughts on Porn

Perspectives, reflections, and critical analysis on pornography and its effects on society

01

The Reality

A Public Health Crisis

Critical

Pornography consumption has reached unprecedented levels. Billions of videos are watched daily, yet the conversation around its harms remains largely absent from mainstream discourse.

The Brain on Porn

Science

Neurological research consistently shows that pornography activates the same dopamine pathways as addictive substances. Repeated exposure rewires reward circuits, diminishing sensitivity to natural stimuli.

Normalization of Harm

Society

What was once considered extreme has become mainstream. This gradual normalization shapes expectations around relationships, consent, and intimacy — especially among younger generations exposed early.

The Industry's Truth

Ethics

Behind the content lies an industry with well-documented exploitation, trafficking, and coercion. Consumption is not passive — it funds and sustains these systems whether users acknowledge it or not.

02

Personal Impact

Intimacy & Relationships

Pornography distorts perceptions of real intimacy, often leading to dissatisfaction, emotional disconnect, and difficulty forming meaningful bonds.

Mental Health

Anxiety, depression, shame, and social isolation are commonly reported among heavy users — often compounding the addictive cycle.

Self-Worth

Constant comparison to unrealistic portrayals erodes confidence and body image, both for consumers and for those who feel measured against it.

Productivity & Focus

Compulsive use disrupts concentration, motivation, and goal-directed behavior — the same mechanisms hijacked by any behavioral addiction.

Sexual Dysfunction

Pornography-induced erectile dysfunction and anorgasmia are increasingly reported, particularly among young men who grew up with high-speed internet access.

Escalation

Tolerance builds over time. Users frequently report needing increasingly extreme content to achieve the same effect — a hallmark pattern of addiction.

What We Believe

—

Pornography is not a harmless pastime. The data is clear, the neuroscience is clear, and the testimonies of those who have struggled are clear.

—

Fighting addiction requires honesty — about the scale of the problem, about who it affects, and about what it costs individuals and society.

—

Recovery is possible. Thousands of people have reclaimed their lives, relationships, and sense of self. It starts with awareness.

—

This site exists because silence is complicity. We track, we analyse, we publish — so the conversation can't be avoided.
